Independent Research

From IS 456 to Eurocode 2: Comparative Design and Finite Element Verification of a Reinforced Concrete Beam

This research investigates the design philosophy differences between Indian Standard IS 456:2000 and Eurocode 2 (EN 1992-1-1) through the detailed study of a singly reinforced concrete beam. The study combines manual analytical design with finite element verification using Abaqus.

The objective was not merely to compare code clauses, but to understand how differences in safety philosophy, partial factors, and serviceability checks influence structural behaviour and design outcomes.

Problem Definition

A simply supported RC beam with defined geometry and loading was selected to ensure a clear comparison between IS and EN provisions.

Finite Element Modelling

The beam was modelled as a 3D solid in Abaqus, discretized using appropriate finite elements, with boundary conditions matching analytical assumptions.

Stress Verification

Von-Mises stress contours were evaluated to verify stress distribution, peak values, and consistency with analytical bending behaviour.

This work strengthened my understanding of design code philosophy, structural behaviour, and the role of numerical verification in modern structural engineering practice — aligning strongly with research-oriented programs such as ACCESS.
