Why ACCESS?

This page explains my academic motivation for applying to the MSc Advanced Computational Civil Engineering Structural Studies (ACCESS) program and how my background aligns with its research-oriented structure.

Academic Motivation

My academic interests have always been strongly connected to mathematics, mechanics, and analytical problem-solving. During my undergraduate studies in Civil Engineering, I developed a particular interest in understanding structural behaviour beyond formula-based design, which motivated me to explore computational and numerical methods.

Through coursework, projects, and independent study, I realized that modern structural engineering increasingly relies on simulation-based analysis to complement traditional code-based design. This realization shaped my decision to pursue advanced training in computational civil engineering.

Preparation for Computational Engineering

During my bachelor’s degree, I completed a 15-week internship at Medini Technologies in association with BIM Construct, where I was trained in tools such as AutoCAD, Revit, STAAD.Pro, Primavera, Lumion, and SketchUp. This exposure helped me understand how digital workflows improve efficiency and accuracy in structural design.

I also gained introductory experience in Python and C programming, which sparked my interest in numerical methods and algorithmic thinking. After graduation, I further strengthened this interest by carrying out an independent mini research project comparing Indian Standards (IS 456) with Eurocode 2 using finite element simulations in Abaqus. This work gave me my first structured exposure to code comparison and verification-based modelling.

Why the ACCESS Program

After evaluating several leading programs in structural and civil engineering, including those at RWTH Aachen and the University of Stuttgart, I found that the ACCESS program at TU Dresden aligns most closely with my academic goals.

The program’s strong emphasis on computational methods, flexible elective structure, and mentoring support provides an ideal environment for refining research interests. Modules related to computational dynamics, bridge design, digital twins, and BIM-based approaches directly match my current academic focus, while still allowing room for exploration and specialization.

Academic and Long-Term Goals

In the short term, my goal is to apply the knowledge gained from ACCESS to computational design and simulation-oriented roles in industry or research institutions. In the long term, I aim to pursue a PhD and develop a career as a researcher and educator in structural engineering.

I am particularly interested in developing code-adaptable and sustainable design methodologies that can be applied across different regulatory environments. I believe the research-oriented training offered by ACCESS provides the right foundation to achieve these goals.
