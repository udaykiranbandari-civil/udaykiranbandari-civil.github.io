Who I Am

I am a civil engineering graduate driven by a deep curiosity for structural behaviour and the mechanics behind design codes. My academic approach emphasizes understanding assumptions, verification of results, and disciplined engineering reasoning.

Rather than applying formulas mechanically, I focus on why structures behave the way they do and how safety philosophies influence design decisions.

Research Philosophy

My research philosophy is built around verification-based engineering. Analytical calculations must be supported by numerical simulations to gain confidence in structural behaviour.

I am particularly interested in the transition from Indian Standards (IS 456) to European design philosophy (Eurocodes), especially in partial safety factors, serviceability checks, and stress distribution.

Research, Projects & Internship

Comparative RC Beam Design (IS 456 vs Eurocode 2)

Independent analytical and numerical study of a singly reinforced concrete beam designed using IS 456:2000 and EN 1992-1-1.

Manual design calculations were followed by finite element verification using Abaqus to study stress distribution and behavioural differences.

RC beam geometry used for analytical comparison

Finite Element Modelling & Verification

The beam was modelled as a 3D solid with appropriate material properties and boundary conditions to reflect analytical assumptions.

Overflow Detection in Drainage Systems (ODDS)

Academic project addressing overflow monitoring in urban drainage systems using sensor-based logic.

Prototype and system workflow developed for overflow detection in urban drainage infrastructure.

Emphasis was placed on problem identification, system logic, and communication of engineering solutions. Awarded 2nd Prize in a technical poster presentation.
