Who I Am

I am a civil engineering graduate driven by a deep curiosity for how structures behave under load and how design codes translate safety philosophy into engineering decisions.

Rather than treating codes as rulebooks, I focus on understanding their assumptions, limitations, and the mechanics behind them. This mindset has guided my academic performance, research work, and project execution throughout my undergraduate studies.

Research Philosophy

My research approach is centered on verification-based engineering. I believe analytical calculations must be supported by numerical validation to fully understand structural behaviour.

I am particularly interested in the transition from Indian Standards (IS 456) to European design philosophy (Eurocodes), focusing on partial safety factors, serviceability criteria, and stress distribution.

Recent Research, Projects & Internship

Comparative RC Beam Design (IS 456 vs Eurocode 2)

Independent analytical and numerical study of a singly reinforced concrete beam designed using IS 456:2000 and EN 1992-1-1.

Manual calculations were carried out for bending and serviceability, followed by finite element verification using Abaqus to study stress distribution and behavioural differences.

RC beam geometry used for analytical comparison

Finite Element Modelling & Verification

The beam was modelled as a 3D solid with appropriate boundary conditions and material properties to reflect analytical assumptions.

Von-Mises stress contours were used to verify bending behaviour and to study how Eurocode partial factors influence stress levels.

Overflow Detection in Drainage Systems (ODDS)

Academic project focused on monitoring overflow conditions in urban drainage systems using sensor-based logic.

The project emphasized problem identification, system logic, and communication of engineering solutions. Awarded 2nd Prize in a technical poster presentation.
