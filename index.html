Research Philosophy

My academic philosophy is rooted in verification-based engineering. Analytical calculations are meaningful only when supported by numerical simulation and behavioural understanding.

I am particularly interested in the transition from Indian Standards (IS 456) to Eurocode-based design philosophy, including differences in partial safety factors, serviceability limits, and stress distribution.

Research, Academic Projects & Internship

Comparative RC Beam Design (IS 456 vs Eurocode 2)

Independent research comparing Indian and European reinforced concrete design philosophies using a simply supported RC beam.

Manual design calculations were carried out under both codes, followed by finite element verification in Abaqus to study stress distribution and structural response.

Finite Element Modelling & Verification

Developed 3D solid finite element models to validate analytical assumptions. Emphasis was placed on boundary conditions, material behaviour, and interpretation of stress contours.

Overflow Detection in Drainage Systems (ODDS)

Academic project addressing real-world urban drainage overflow issues through sensor-based monitoring logic.
