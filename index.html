Research, Academic Projects and Internship

Overflow Detection in Drainage Systems

This project addressed urban drainage overflow during high intensity rainfall events. A sensor based monitoring concept was developed to detect abnormal water levels within drainage channels and trigger early alerts.

The work focused on preventive infrastructure monitoring rather than post event inspection. It emphasized practical application of drainage principles, system integration, and clear technical communication.

Sustainable Construction Materials Seminar

This seminar examined the use of recycled aggregates, bamboo, timber, and other renewable resources in building construction. The study focused on material performance, sustainability, and environmental impact.

Key considerations included strength variability, durability, design limitations, and appropriate structural applications. The work strengthened my understanding of sustainable material selection and life cycle thinking in civil engineering.

Extensive Survey Project

A large scale field survey covering existing and proposed tanks, highways, water supply systems, and town planning layouts. Measurements were carried out using Total Station and processed into technical drawings and layouts.

This project provided hands on exposure to field accuracy, data processing, coordination, and real world engineering constraints.

Industry Internship at Medini Technologies

A fifteen week internship focused on AutoCAD drafting, BIM workflows, and professional engineering documentation. The internship strengthened my understanding of industry standards and engineering practice.
